PlayStation's Next Console Isn't a Box — It's a Handheld

By TriHard Gaming Tech · July 20, 2026 · 7 min read

Sony's next PlayStation generation may launch as a handheld device you can dock to your TV. We cross-referenced five sources to build this picture of what's coming.

The Core Intel

Multiple sources now confirm Sony is developing a dockable PS6 handheld[1] with a target release window of Fall 2027. The details, sourced from hardware insider Moore's Law Is Dead who reviewed up-to-date AMD documentation[2], paint a specific picture:

Spec Detail
GPU16 CU RDNA 5 iGPU
Clock (Handheld)1.20 GHz
Clock (Docked)1.65 GHz
Backwards CompatiblePS4 & PS5 games
Estimated Price$399–$499

Three Consoles, One Generation

Here's what's most interesting: Sony appears to be launching three devices simultaneously in Fall 2027[2]:

  • PS6S — A budget, digital-only model (~$299–$399)
  • PS6 Handheld — The dockable hybrid (~$399–$499)
  • PS6 — The standard home console (~$549–$699)

This is a Nintendo Switch 2 strategy: cover every price bracket with one hardware architecture.

Why Handheld First?

Two data points stand out from our research:

1. PlayStation Portal Evolution. Sony shipped the PlayStation Portal in 2023 as a streaming-only remote player. But if the PS6 handheld runs PS5 and PS6 titles natively (no streaming required), that's a fundamental architectural shift. The Portal was a proof of concept; the handheld is the product.

2. The Xbox Precedent. Microsoft launched the ROG Xbox Ally and Xbox Ally X (releasing October 2026)[1]. Sony's handheld targets "significantly less" pricing with "way better" age characteristics. At $399–$499, it directly competes with Nintendo Switch 2 territory while undercutting Xbox's PC-based handhelds.

The Disc Question

Former PlayStation CEO Shawn Layden said in February 2026 that Sony "can't get away" with a discless launch[3]. His reasoning: Xbox succeeds digitally in English-speaking markets, but Sony leads in 170+ countries including regions with slower internet infrastructure.

Our read: the PS6S will likely be digital-only, but the PS6 handheld will have a disc slot for the dock — or at least the base PS6 will. Sony knows global markets still buy physical games.

TriHard Analysis

Three simultaneous console launches is bold but risky. The PS5 shipped November 2020, seven years after PS4. If PS6 hits Fall 2027, that's ~7 years — consistent with Sony's cadence. The real question: will the handheld cannibalize the home console? Nintendo Switch sold 14M+ units. If the PS6 handheld captures even half that market at $400+, it's $2.8 billion in hardware revenue alone — and the game ecosystem compounds from there.

What Games Are Waiting?

Two confirmed PS6-era titles already exist:

  • Physint — Hideo Kojima's return to "action espionage" (Metal Gear's DNA)
  • The Witcher 4 — targeting 2027 release, likely PS6-native

Both suggest a PS6 launch window of late 2027, aligning with Moore's Law Is Dead's Fall 2027 estimate.

The Bottom Line

Sony's PS6 strategy mirrors Nintendo's best play: hybrid hardware at accessible pricing, backed by exclusive software. If the handheld delivers on the specs (16 CU RDNA 5, dockable, PS4/PS5 backwards compatible), it's not just a competitor — it's a category-defining product that could reshape the entire console market.

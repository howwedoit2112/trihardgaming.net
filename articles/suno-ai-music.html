Suno AI: How Music Is Being Made (and Stolen) by AI

Terrence O'Brien Jul 18, 2026 Source: The Verge

Suno AI has quietly become one of the most popular music generation tools on the planet. The startup's models can produce full songs — vocals, instruments, and arrangement — from simple text prompts in seconds.

How Suno Works

Suno's models were trained on millions of songs scraped from YouTube, Genius, and Deezer. The scraped audio includes decades of recorded music, giving the AI a vast library of styles and sounds to learn from.

Key Stories

  • Training data: Suno ripped audio from YouTube, Genius, and Deezer to train its models
  • Copyright flood: AI-generated covers of Beyoncé and other artists are flooding streaming platforms
  • Country music: Nashville writing rooms are adopting AI tools for song composition

"The future of country music is here, and it's AI. Nashville's writing rooms will never be the same."

— Charlie Harding, The Verge

The AI Music Pipeline

The workflow is simple: type a prompt like "upbeat country song about a broken-down truck in Texas," and Suno generates a complete song with vocals in under 30 seconds. The quality is impressive enough that many listeners can't tell the difference between AI-generated and human-made music.
